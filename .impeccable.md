## Design Context

### Users
Swiss KMUs (small and medium enterprises) who have received outreach from Simon Kröner and are
googling him to verify credibility before deciding whether to engage. Desktop-first evaluation
context — they are assessing whether Simon is professional, capable, and trustworthy. Not cold
traffic; warm leads making a judgment call.

### Brand Personality
Direct, "Macher" (Swiss German: doer/builder), growth-focused.
The site should read like someone who picks up the phone, delivers work, and gets results —
not like a design studio portfolio.

### Aesthetic Direction
Same exact color palette as Kröner & Starke (kroener-starke.ch): violet #7c3aed, cream #f8f6f2,
ink #12100e. But must feel distinctly personal, not like a copy of the partnership site.

Key differentiators from KSWD:
- Typography: KSWD uses Inter (polished, neutral). Simon uses Barlow Condensed (display) +
  Hanken Grotesk (body) — assertive, direct, Swiss-industrial character
- Background treatment: Clean light sections (no decorative violet blobs). Dark sections keep
  the violet atmosphere. The contrast is intentional — restraint in light, drama in dark.
- Less round: Tighter border radii for a more precise, less "soft studio" feel
- Process numbers: More prominent, making the maker's workflow feel like the real product

Theme: Light mode (matches client evaluation context — professional, daytime use).

### Design Principles
1. **Direct over decorative** — every visual element must earn its place. No ambient decoration.
2. **Typography does the heavy lifting** — Barlow Condensed Black at large sizes creates
   immediate personality without needing graphic embellishments.
3. **Drama only where it counts** — the dark violet sections (hero, CTA) carry the brand energy.
   Light sections are clean and readable.
4. **Same palette, different voice** — identical colors to KSWD but used with a different
   typographic register. Visitors who know KSWD should feel the family resemblance, but
   Simon's site should feel unmistakably personal.
5. **Built for credibility** — spacing, alignment, and precision signal that Simon's work is
   as careful as his presentation.
